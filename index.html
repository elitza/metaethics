Metaethics

http://metaethics.org

  1. Everything is a structure, almost everything contains structures, is embedded in structures and is part of structures. You are a structure, your family is a structure, society, country, the human race… everything.
  2. When a change strengthens a structure, that's what we call "good for [that structure]". If the strengthened structure feels highly relevant to us we simply call this kind of change "good". When a change weakens a structure, that's what we call "bad for [that structure]". If the weakened structure feels highly relevant to us we simply call this kind of change "bad".
  3. The more relevant a structure feels to us, the more any strengthening of that structure appears "good" – and any weakening of that structure appears "evil".
  4. To make a person desire a certain change either show that it strengthens a structure that is relevant to them, or make a structure that is strengthened by that change relevant to them. To make a person fear a change either show that it weakens a structure that is relevant to them, or make a structure that is weakened by that change relevant to them.
  5. The feeling of relevancy towards structures is innate, as are the mechanisms by which structures become relevant.
  6. Some structures actually are relevant to us and our species in a physical way, like environment and reproduction. Others we depend on in more abstract ways, like order, authority or good argumentation. Others yet feel relevant by "mirroring", like the characters in a story or people we are told about on the news. Some structures are only ideas or non-referring concepts that we feel to be relevant to us for different reasons.
  7. Likely, you will find what some call "inner peace" by aligning all your relevant structures in a concentric way, thus that the inner, closer structures support the larger, encompassing structures.
  8. Likely, you will find happiness by giving yourself up to the structure that feels most relevant to you – while not hurting any other relevant structure.
  9. Likely, you will be considered a "good" human being if you help other human beings to align and support their relevant structures.
  10. Don't act as you want others to act towards you, that's selfish. Don't act in a way that should become a general rule, that's self-delusional. Act in a way that to the best of your knowledge supports all relevant structures.
